How to play

To play, just choose the "Play" option from the main menu and pick an area, really.

In-game, you are allowed to control the leaders and play around like in the canon Pikmin games. Depending on the area, you can either explore at your heart's content, or must complete a mission, usually with some goal, fail conditions, and scoring criteria. You can check the controls by going to the options menu from the main menu, or by reading the help menu.

The minimum requirements to run the engine aren't that high; old machines should run it just fine without many performance problems. But it does depend on what is going on: a simple area with just a leader will run much better than a complex area with 100 Pikmin on the field. Some options can be set to help with performance, at the cost of appearance, while others just tweak the experience based on your preferences.

Though the idea of Pikifen is for you to create your own content, you can still play around with the base content that comes with the engine. Because that content is based on the most important things in the canon games, it's normal to have some of it present in content you or others make.

Pikmin gameplay

It's strongly recommended that you play the official Pikmin games, both to support Nintendo, and to more easily understand what to do in Pikifen. The gameplay concepts, mechanics, and rules can be figured out by experimenting, playing the official games, or by reading the the help menu.

The gameplay mechanics apply to the entirety of Pikifen, regardless of what content you're playing, though some content may have more unusual goals or gimmicks, and require you to play a bit differently.

Misc.

  • Press F1 (by default) during gameplay to toggle the system info visualizer.
  • Press F12 (by default) to take a screenshot. It gets saved in the user_data folder.
  • When you end a mission, your result will get saved automatically if it's a new record.
